Large Tech Client

Email App - Innovation - Consultation

Problem

My client's stakeholders wanted the product to be more innovative, so my client hired me to present a new take on a classic email client to give them fresh ideas. Additionally, a persona for them based off of research conducted through a survey to allow them to focus their efforts on their customer.

Process

I did video conferences with my clients to clarify the scope of the project and give regular updates. I opened the door for innovation by getting to the core of what an email client is trying to accomplish in the workplace and applying that to my new project design.

Results

I did a presentation to my client's stakeholders so they could ask questions about the different ideas that I proposed with my designs. They took the general philosophy and some of the smaller features that could be accomplished in their timeline and applied them moving forward.



Consultation Objectives

  1. Conduct a user survey.

  2. Create a persona for this product.

  3. Design an innovative alternative to the typical email client.

  4. Discuss the ideas with the client and their stakeholders.

User Research

I conducted a user survey with business people asking more generally about how they prefer to communicate in the workplace, what programs they prefer, their experiences with email clients, and what kind of communication/collaboration application would they create if they had a magic wand.

Findings:

  1. People use a variety of platforms to communicate.

  2. Most prefer custom organization of their files.

  3. Finding old files is a major pain point.

  4. Most would like to have their applications integrated in one place.

  5. People would like the information to be cross-platform
